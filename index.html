Oil-Drizzled Noodles

Oil-Drizzled Noodles is a signature dish from Shaanxi Province, China. It features hand-rolled noodles topped with various ingredients, then finished with a splash of hot oil to release its aromatic flavors. Tangy and spicy, it's perfect for everyday cooking.

Required ingredients

  • Knife-cut noodles, 200g
  • Green vegetables, 200g
  • Chili powder, 2 tablespoons
  • Soy sauce, 2 tablespoons
  • chopped green onions, a little
  • Minced garlic, a little
  • cooking oil, 1 tablespoon

Cooking Steps

  1. Wash the green vegetables and set them aside.
  2. Pour 500g of water into a pot. Once boiling, add the noodles and greens. Cook for 4 minutes, then drain and serve.
  3. Drain the water from the noodles, then add soy sauce, chili powder, chopped scallions, and minced garlic. Set aside.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of oil to the pot. Once hot, pour it over the noodles.
  5. Add the reserved green vegetables and stir until evenly combined.

Quick Tip

If you prefer a sour and spicy flavor, add 1 spoon of vinegar when seasoning. Reducing the cooking time slightly will make the noodles more chewy. Serve immediately after preparation, otherwise the noodles will clump together and lose their texture.
